Executive Summary

This week's index update, based on GDELT, ACLED, World Bank, IMF, and satellite data processed through April 12, 2026, reveals three distinct escalation patterns operating simultaneously across different geopolitical theatres: a structural break in Nigeria's military risk indices, a dual-index surge in Iraq, and the sudden emergence of elevated coup risk in both Armenia and Tunisia.

Africa leads all regions in composite risk score (0.078), with Europe/Russia at 0.065 and the Americas at 0.064. The Middle East shows an unusual de-escalation signal (-0.053 from last week), driven by Gulf corridor dynamics.

1. Nigeria: CRITICAL Threshold Breached

Nigeria's Military Escalation index reached 0.784 on April 12 — the highest reading in our current dataset and a crossing of what we define as the CRITICAL threshold (>0.600). One week prior, the index stood at 0.115. The absolute change of +0.669 points over seven days is the largest single-week movement in any country-topic pair this week.

The Military Clash index moved in parallel, reaching 0.743 — also CRITICAL.

In NERAI's framework, a simultaneous surge in both Military Escalation and Military Clash indices indicates that the escalation is not anticipatory (reflected only in escalation signals) but actively kinetic (confirmed in clash events captured by ACLED and GDELT). When these two indices diverge, it often signals anticipated rather than actual conflict; when they converge at elevated levels, it indicates active engagement.

12-Month Forecast: Our Prophet model projects Nigeria's military risk trajectory remains elevated through Q3 2026, with the 12-month average below current peak — indicating the model expects some mean-reversion but not a full de-escalation.

2. Iraq: Dual-Index Structural Break

Iraq's risk profile changed materially this week across two independent indices:

| Index | April 5 | April 12 | Change |

|-------|---------|---------|--------|

| Coup Risk | 0.062 | 0.442 | +0.380 pts [HIGH] |

| Political Instability | 0.048 | 0.472 | +0.423 pts [HIGH] |

Both indices are derived from separate event streams within our data pipeline. Coup Risk draws primarily on GDELT's political classification categories and ACLED's political violence event types; Political Instability uses a broader composite incorporating governance indicators from World Bank datasets. When both move in tandem with magnitudes of this size, NERAI's causal network classifies the pattern as systemic rather than noise.

Iraq's Coup Risk reaching 0.442 places it in HIGH territory — a level historically associated with periods of significant political stress and leadership uncertainty.

3. New Signal Emergence: Armenia and Tunisia

Two countries crossed from near-zero into elevated risk territory this week — a pattern our model treats as a distinct category from ongoing escalations:

Armenia — Coup Risk: 0.000 → 0.247 [ELEVATED]

Armenia's Coup Risk index was essentially zero through early April. A score of 0.247 in the ELEVATED range indicates a meaningful clustering of political instability and leadership pressure events in the GDELT data, confirmed by ACLED domestic political crisis classifications.

Tunisia — Coup Risk: 0.000 → 0.303 [ELEVATED]

Tunisia's reading of 0.303 is higher and more concerning — placing it at the upper end of the ELEVATED range. The simultaneous emergence of Political Instability (0.343, ELEVATED) and Ethnic/Religious Violence (0.152, MODERATE) indices suggests a multi-dimensional stress pattern.

In our historical analysis, "sudden emergence" signals — defined as a country crossing from below 0.02 to above 0.10 within a 7-day window — have shown stronger predictive validity for near-term political events than gradual-escalation patterns. This is counterintuitive but consistent: sudden breaks from near-zero often reflect a real-world threshold crossing that the data captures with a lag, then all at once.

4. Russia's Underreported Trajectory

While Ukraine dominates the military risk headlines (Military Escalation: 0.643 [CRITICAL], Military Clash: 0.559 [HIGH]), Russia's 12-month forecast trends deserve equal attention:

  • Political Instability: Rising trajectory, +18.2% 12-month trend
  • International Crisis: Rising trajectory, +35.1% 12-month trend

Russia's current Political Instability index is at 0.185 — elevated but not extreme. However, the directional signal from our Prophet model is consistently upward, and International Crisis at 0.106 with a +35% rising trend represents one of the stronger forward-looking signals in the European theatre.
