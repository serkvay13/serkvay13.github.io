NERAI Risk Pulse — Week 19: India-Pakistan Standoff Dominates, Iran Recalibrates, Israel Governance Crisis Deepens

📅 May 10, 2026 📊 NERAI Strategic Insights Hub 🌍 60 Countries · 40 Dimensions

Executive Summary

Week 19 (May 4–9, 2026) marks a structural shift in the global risk landscape. The dominant escalation signal has migrated from the Iran-Israel axis — which drove W18 — to the India-Pakistan military corridor, where NERAI's Military Escalation Index for Pakistan (PK) reached 84.6/100 on the 12-month normalized scale, while the Kashmir sub-index (KS) posted the single largest weekly surge in the active dataset at +35.2 points week-on-week. Simultaneously, Iran's military escalation has entered a recalibration phase — still at 82.5/100, but now on a confirmed falling trend (-5.6% 12m). Israel's internal fracture deepens: Government Instability at 98.2/100 and rising is the highest reading across all 60 countries and 40 NERAI dimensions.

W19 Top Signal: Kashmir Military Escalation +35.2 pts W-o-W (largest single-week spike in dataset) · Pakistan Mil. Escalation 84.6/100 · India Military Crisis 70.5/100 · Israel Gov. Instability 98.2/100 (rising) · Russia Mil. Escalation 93.0/100 (stable high)

2 — Iran: Post-Peak Recalibration

After registering maximum intensity (Military Escalation 100.0/100) in Week 18, Iran's W19 data reveals a bifurcated signal. Weekly event volume remains elevated — the W19 weekly mean ticked up to 61.6/100 — but the 12-month forecast-weighted current value stands at 82.5/100 with a confirmed falling trend (-5.6%). Government Instability fell sharply (trend: -10.4%), while Political Instability is also declining (-6.4%). This pattern is textbook post-peak consolidation: external military signaling is moderating, but internal structural pressures remain embedded.
