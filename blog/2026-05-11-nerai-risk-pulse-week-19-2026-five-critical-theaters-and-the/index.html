This week's NERAI Risk Pulse identifies a bifurcated risk landscape: five active conflict theaters holding CRITICAL-level Military Escalation scores simultaneously, while a cluster of Western democracies registers the most significant incremental movements in their instability indices in recent weeks.

Five Concurrent CRITICAL Military Theaters

As of the April 10 data update — the most recent index cycle — NERAI's Military Escalation Index flags five countries at CRITICAL level (≥0.90 on a 0–1 normalized scale):

  • Ukraine: 0.971 — the highest military escalation score across all 60 monitored countries
  • Afghanistan: 0.959 — sustained at near-maximum, with Instability also at CRITICAL (0.901)
  • Syria: 0.949 — Political Instability concurrently elevated at 0.859 (CRITICAL)
  • Russia: 0.936 — compounding with Ukraine's score to reflect an active bilateral conflict theater
  • Iraq: 0.900 — Military Escalation at threshold, Government Instability elevated

Iran's Government Instability reads 0.871 (CRITICAL), and both Ukraine and Afghanistan carry CRITICAL Instability scores above 0.87. The concentration of CRITICAL-level readings across multiple dimensions and multiple countries simultaneously is statistically unusual in our dataset.

The Week's Most Significant Movements

While the CRITICAL theaters attract attention, the sharpest movement this week occurred elsewhere:

Switzerland recorded a +0.109 point single-week increase in Government Instability (0.059 → 0.168) — its largest recorded weekly movement and a jump from LOW to MODERATE classification. This coincides with a 12-month trend signal of +150% on Protest Activity for Switzerland, the highest directional trend in the current dataset.

The United States registered +0.073 points on Instability (0.166 → 0.239, now ELEVATED) and +0.066 on Political Instability (to 0.260). The US Military Escalation metric carries a +40% 12-month trend, consistent with a structural, not episodic, shift in the signal environment.

France moved +0.054 on Political Instability (to 0.221) and +0.033 on Military Escalation (to 0.181), while its Protest Activity trend is running at +48% on the 12-month model. Canada advanced +0.036 on Political Instability (to 0.167), carrying a +33% trend.

Methodology Note

NERAI indices are constructed from daily signal ingestion across GDELT (event data), ACLED (conflict events), World Bank and IMF economic indicators, and satellite-derived mobility and infrastructure signals. Scores represent normalized probabilistic risk levels (0.00–1.00) derived from causal network models spanning 2,400+ country-topic interaction pairs. 12-month trend projections are generated via Prophet-based time-series models fit independently per country-topic pair.

Scores are updated daily. This briefing reflects the April 10, 2026 index cycle.
